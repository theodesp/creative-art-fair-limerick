About

The Expressive Arts Fair was found by Georgie Vargemezi, visual artist, as a result of seeing that festivals in Limerick were focused on singular artforms. She decided to create and coordinate a Fair where as many artforms as possible come together under one roof with the view of multidisciplinary collaborations igniting, visitors being inspired by different artistic processes, and artists of all artforms celebrating their work. The Expressive Arts Fair will include artist workshops, demonstrations, plenty of artist stalls and artworks for sale, and a central garden with a popup non-alcoholic bar by 101 Limerick.


Georgie Vargemezi currently works from Wickham studios, Limerick, and has a B.A. (Hons) from the University of York, UK, in History of Art. She has worked within the community, and with local Limerick charities for the last 3 years, with art and gardening workshops and demonstrations. Georgie now holds abstract art workshops for adults & children, and is an independent exhibition curator. You can find her on Instagram at @georgievargemezi. Whether you are an individual, a group, or an organisation, and you are interested in taking part, please click on the link below, and get in touch.

Milk Market Limerick

The Expressive Arts Fair is taking place in the famous Limerick Milk Market, which has been operating since 1852. David Fitzgerald, the manager of the market, has been and is an integral part of the Expressive Arts Fair materialising from plan to reality. The Milk Market operates on Fridays, Saturdays & Sundays with a cafe culture, a farmer's market, live music & yoga on Sundays.

Non Alcoholic Bar by 101 Limerick

101 Limerick, a fabulous cocktail bar in Limerick city, will have a popup, non-alcoholic bar in the middle garden area in the Milk Market during the whole day of the Expressive Arts Fair. 101 Limerick are the Southern Comfort People's Choice Bar of the Year 2019. Take time out from workshops, demonstrations, and viewing amazing artworks and try them out.
